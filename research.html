Research

Scattering amplitudes and Feynman integrals, across two related directions.

Cosmology & the EFT of Large-Scale Structure

I work on precision predictions for large-scale structure observables within the Effective Field Theory of Large-Scale Structure (EFTofLSS), which organizes the perturbative expansion of cosmological correlators in a controlled way. My focus is on computing higher-loop corrections to these observables using techniques from the scattering amplitudes and Feynman integrals literature.

In my Master's thesis, I computed the two-loop matter power spectrum, working through the relevant loop integrals and their reduction in the EFTofLSS framework.

Scattering amplitudes, positive geometries & surfaceology

On the more formal side, I am interested in the mathematical structures underlying scattering amplitudes — in particular positive geometries and the emerging framework of surfaceology. Starting this autumn, this will be the focus of my PhD at the Max Planck Institute for Physics, supervised by Prof. Dr. Johannes Henn and co-supervised by Prof. Lorenzo Tancredi.
