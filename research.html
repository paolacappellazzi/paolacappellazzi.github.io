Research

Current Interests

Broadly, scattering amplitudes and Feynman integrals, across two related directions:
  • cosmology & EFT of Large-Scale Structure: precision predictions for large-scale structure observables, in particular computing higher-loop corrections using techniques from the scattering amplitudes and Feynman integrals literature
  • scattering amplitudes: more formal aspects, especially positive geometries and the emerging framework of surfaceology

Master's Thesis

My Master's thesis, in the Quantum Field Theory group at the Max Planck Institute for Physics (supervised by Prof. Dr. Johannes Henn and Dr. Fabian Schmidt), is on the two-loop matter power spectrum within the EFT of Large-Scale Structure — working through the relevant loop integrals and their evaluation in this framework.

PhD, starting autumn 2026

This autumn I will begin a PhD at the Max Planck Institute for Physics, supervised by Prof. Dr. Johannes Henn and co-supervised by Prof. Lorenzo Tancredi (TUM), moving towards the formal side of scattering amplitudes — specifically positive geometries and surfaceology.
